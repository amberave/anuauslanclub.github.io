# Welcome to the ANU Auslan Club Website!

For the latest updates on the ANU Auslan Club, please follow our [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/anuauslanclub/) and [Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/482736490736658/).

### What is Auslan?
Auslan is **Aus**tralian Sign **Lan**guage.

### What is the ANU Auslan Club?
Our club was founded in 2023 by Dr Gemma King and a group of students at ANU, to provide financially-accessible Auslan classes to ANU students and uplift students' understanding of Auslan and Deaf culture. Dr King is currently leading the project [Sign on Screen](https://signonscreen.com/) which studies the representation of sign language in cinema and television. The website includes a database which allows you to browse and search films that include representation of sign language.

## Learning Auslan
### Classes through ANU Auslan Club
Unfortunately, the club is not currently offering classes. Any future offerings will be advertised on our Instagram and Facebook group.

### Local Classes
The best, most effective way to learn Auslan is face-to-face classes. 

Deaf Connect offer classes for a concession fee or partial subsidy for eligible students:
* [Beginner classes](https://deafconnect.org.au/services/beginner-courses-in-auslan) run for a term and are a good way to learn some basic conversational Auslan.
* [Certificate II in Auslan](https://deafconnect.org.au/course/certificate-ii-in-auslan) is available to complete beginners, and offers a more in-depth class with assessments to test your knowledge and understanding of class content.
### Other Methods and Resources
Although we strongly recommend you learn face-to-face with a certified Auslan teacher, we understand that some students cannot afford these classes or do not have the availability to commit to weekly classes. Below are some methods and resources that can help get you started on your Auslan journey!

**Online Course through La Trobe University (that can count towards your degree!)**

La Trobe University offers Auslan courses. We have had a member of our executive team complete one of these courses and have it successfully added to their academic record as an elective. This process can be found here [LINK]

**Other Online Courses**

[Deaf Connect](https://deafconnect.org.au/course/sign-online) offers a self-paced online course. There are also many other Auslan courses available online that you can research to find one that suits your needs. 

**Free Resources**

There are also free resources available if you want to learn a few signs on your own and/or are not able to attend classes.
* [Auslan Signbank](https://auslan.org.au/) is like an online dictionary of signs! Each entry has a short video demonstrating the sign, definition/s where it is used, and a map of Australia to show in which states the sign is used.
* [Asphyxia's Auslan 1 Online Course](https://helloasphyxia.wordpress.com/blog/learn-auslan-australian-sign-language-online-course/) - Asphyxia is a Deaf activist and author who kindly uploaded a series of lessons on YouTube to teach basic signs. This can be a good starting point to teach yourself the basics.
